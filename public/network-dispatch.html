How the MVP Works

1. Operator Sends Offer

If the fleet is full, the operator sends a dispatch offer into the network.

2. Qualified Partner Accepts

The first eligible partner to accept wins the ride.

3. Assignment Is Locked

The system marks the dispatch as assigned and closes it for everyone else.

4. Workflow Gets Triggered

The accepted dispatch is pushed into the partner operator's workflow/webhook for handling.

Can It Use the Existing SaaS Workflow?

Yes, in most cases it can. The easiest MVP is to send the accepted dispatch into the same inbound workflow structure the partner already uses, as long as the payload includes a dispatch flag and the workflow can branch correctly.

Recommended Workflow Flag

Include a field like booking_source = network_dispatch or dispatch_partner_assigned = true so the partner workflow knows this is not a direct self-booked customer.

What the Partner Workflow Should Do

Create or update the contact, create the opportunity, assign the trip internally, and trigger the right partner-side notifications without needing a cross-account calendar write in MVP.

Customer Transparency

If a ride is assigned through the dispatch network, the customer should be clearly informed that a trusted network partner operator will be providing service on your behalf.

Suggested customer-facing wording

Your reservation may be fulfilled by a trusted network partner operator on our behalf. If assigned to a partner, all service standards and trip details will remain managed through our network.
